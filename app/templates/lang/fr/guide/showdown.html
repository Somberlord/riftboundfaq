Showdown

Showdown occurs when a battlefield (BF) becomes contested

  • If the BF is uncontrolled, an open showdown without combat occurs
  • If the BF is controlled by another player, Combat occurs with showdown as a part of it
  • If the showdown is part of combat, the attacker and defender are the relevant players
    • Others may become relevant if triggers they control happen during showdown or if they are invited by a relevant player
    • The attacker is the controller of the unit that applied the contested status to the BF
  • If the showdown is not part of combat, all players are relevant players

Visualisation of Showdown: Link

If the showdown is part of combat, it may begin with an initial chain:

  • "When I attack" triggers are put on the chain first
  • "When I defend" triggers are put on top
  • Players may react

After the initial chain is resolved, focus goes to the player that contested the BF. A player with focus may do one of the following things:

  • Play an action or reaction spell
  • Activate legally timed abilities
    • Cards or abilities will start a chain, at the end of which focus will pass to the next relevant player
  • Invite players to act
    • Invitations may be refused
    • If accepted, the invited player gains focus and must play a spell or activate an ability right away
  • Pass focus

When all relevant players have passed in succession, showdown ends and a cleanup occurs. If there was no combat and the unit is still at the BF, it is conquered

  • If the player has already scored a point on that BF this turn (for example by holding it), the BF is not conquered again. No point is awarded and "When you conquer" triggers do not trigger.
